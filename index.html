10 Tips to Help You Sell Your House Fast

Looking for ways to quickly and successfully sell your home? Here are ten tips for selling your house fast:

1. Prep Your Home: Property buyers are picky and so it’s important to ensure your house stands out from the crowd. Ensure all repairs are done, surfaces are dusted, carpets shampooed and all improvements you have thought of are complete. Always keep in mind the importance of curb appeal and don’t let prospective buyers drive away unattracted.

2.Hiring an Agent: When it comes to selling a home, an agent with expertise in your area is key. Your agent will provide resources and advice as well as stage your home to appeal to the most buyers, direct them to your listing and handle all the back-and-forth negotiations for you.

3. Make Room: Oftentimes buyers are looking for a house to move into immediately, so don’t leave it cluttered. Remove excess furniture, cords, and items to help a buyer envision themselves in their home. Box up items that would be in their way and store them away until you’re ready to move out.

4. Take Professional Pictures: Professional photos are a must! A buyer may not even take a look at your home if they find the pictures uninviting. Clean everything in the pictures and make sure all the small details are in order.

5. Plan Your Open House Strategically: Plan your open house carefully and focus on picking a good time of day. Weekdays between 10 and 4 pm is ideal as many buyers can’t make it on weekends. Furthermore, make sure you de-clutter the house area and make it inviting.

6. Learn the Market: Do your homework and get to know what type of offers buyers in your area are making. Are you up against investors looking for a “fixer-upper” or are buyers looking for a more updated aesthetic?

7. Invest in Social Media: Platforms such as Facebook and Instagram can show sellers a different side to selling their home. Post your listing with captivating pictures and feature it in a story to reach more people. Encourage followers to share the post with friends who may also be interested.

8. Price to Sell: Instead of pricing at the market value, ensure you have left some room for potential buyers to feel as though they got a good deal — it will make them more likely to submit an offer. You can also do this by pricing it a little below the market value.

9. Don’t forget the touchups: Put together a list of home improvement projects that need to get done and make sure to get them done by the time buyers come in for the tour. For a buyer’s point of view, these touch-ups could help envision a new life in the space.

10. Get Creative: There are many pricing strategies you can use to attract more buyers to your house. Offer discounts to certain groups of people, offer buyer incentives such as providing new appliances or helping to cover closing costs.

Prep Your Home

Getting your home ready to sell is one of the most important steps in the process. Keeping it in show-ready condition, inside and out, can be the difference between an over-asking offer and no offers. Curb appeal is key, and should be the first thing to focus on. Mow the lawn, clean the gutters, and plant some new flowers to give a great first impression. Inside, de-clutter, patch up any holes or scratches, and make sure the walls, floors, and fixtures are all sparkling clean.

If there are big projects to take on, such as replacing flooring, painting, or even outdoor repairs, go ahead and get those done. Buyers will pay more for a house that looks move-in ready, and ones that need major upgrades may sit on the market for a while.

If you don’t want to make any big investments for repairs, there are many low-cost, high-impact DIY projects that can add lots of value without breaking the bank. Repaint, switch out hardware and light fixtures, refinish countertops, and clean up the landscaping. Even deep-cleaning carpets can really make a difference!

Also, keep in mind how different buyers may see your home and make it as appealing as possible to the largest possible audience. Depersonalize the décor to make the place look more generic, and add little touches like fresh flowers or a brightly colored throw cushion to make the space look airy and inviting.

Hire the Right Agent

If you’re looking to get the most bang for your buck, it might be worth bringing in a real estate agent to help sell your house. A real estate agent can help guide owners through the tricky process of pricing, negotiating, and closing a sale. The right agent can help maximize profits by finding the right buyers in the right time frame.

Agents are typically well-connected in their local communities, meaning they can often attract shoppers more quickly than owners can reach alone. They also add an element of distance and professionalism to house shopping, making the process less stressful for both buyers and sellers.

When hiring an agent, look for one that focuses on your area. Make sure to get referrals from those you know, and interview a few different options to find the best one that works for you. Know that, in most cases, agents will collect a commission of about 6% of the sales price, so be mindful of that when you decide how to list, how to price, and when to accept or refuse an offer.

Make Room and Take Professional Photos

One of the best ways to attract buyers is to make sure the house is clean and tidy. That’s why it’s super important to de-clutter and make room. Don’t leave furniture in awkward places and make sure to remove any extra cords. You don’t want buyers to feel cramped when they enter the house.

Besides de-cluttering, hiring a professional photographer can make all the difference. As the saying goes, “first impressions last”, and the majority of buyers will take a look at photos before deciding if they’d like to take a look at the house in person. This means you have to make sure those pictures pop!

Consider hiring an experienced photographer to shoot the place. Someone who knows how to capture the glow of natural light and showcase all the highlights of the home will be the best fit. Ask your agent for recommendations, and invest in taking some great photos that will increase the chances of your home selling.

Plan Open Houses Strategically

When it comes time to show off the home to potential buyers, open houses are the way to go. They are an excellent and efficient way to get many people through your home in a short period of time, and this is key for a quick sale.

When planning an open house, pick a good time and day for it. Try to avoid weekends, and instead host an open house on Tuesdays through Thursdays between 10am and 4pm. That way, potential buyers who are the busiest during weekends, can still make it during their lunch or after-work free time.

Another thing to keep in mind is that buyers like to have privacy when they wander around the house. So, if you can, try to be present at the open house but stay in the background while they take a look inside. The same goes for your real estate agent, too.

Additionally, don’t forget to de-clutter and spruce up the open house. Place flowers around the house and make sure to clean out cobwebs and dust. Remember, the end goal is to make buyers feel happy and eager to make an offer.

Learn the Market and Invest in Social Media

Doing your research about the market will make all the difference when it comes to getting the most money for your home. Familiarizing yourself with house prices, likes and dislikes from buyers, and competition in your area will be extremely helpful.

Another great idea is to invest in social media. Create ads on Facebook, Instagram, and other popular platforms and post photos of your house. You can also reduce the price for buyers who share your post on their wall and encourage people to repost it.

The power of social media can be a great asset. Just keep in mind to make the post captivating and use clear and enticing pictures. If more people learn about your house, the more chances you have of selling it faster.

Price to Sell and Get Creative

When offering your home, be sure to make it attractive to potential buyers. Setting the right price is one of the key factors in selling your house quickly, so be mindful. You can go a little lower than the market value to give buyers more motivation to make an offer.

In addition, don’t be afraid to get creative. Offer discounts for certain types of buyers like first-time homebuyers or if someone is willing to move in within a short period of time. Also, consider offering incentives, such as covering closing costs, buying appliances, or even paying a bit of the buyer’s mortgage.

When it comes to selling your house, remember to stay positive and be confident that you will get the best deal. Focus on these tips, get creative, and be open to ideas. With a bit of luck and effort, you’ll be able to sell your house fast!
